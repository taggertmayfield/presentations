The rule that makes it work: the panelization discipline extends to air. Each zone heats and cools itself with its own slim ducted unit; the only things crossing zone lines are ⅜"/⅝" refrigerant linesets and 4–6" ERV duct, and those run in a sealed, conditioned crawl — skirt panels off the same jig table close the pier perimeter, so the whole underfloor becomes the service chase this no-attic house doesn't otherwise have. The exposed glulams and sloped T&G ceilings are never touched.
H1

System Section · Through Zone B

CONDITIONED CRAWL = SERVICE CHASE

Zone B is the proof case: its unit hangs horizontally in the crawl under the kitchen, feeding floor supplies at the base of the 15' glass wall — heat exactly where the biggest heat loss lives, washing the glazing — with a floor return near the island. Zones A and C use the same unit mounted in a 7'-6" dropped ceiling over their rear service bands (bath/WIC in A, pantry/laundry in C), so every unit is a filter-change away and no finished ceiling drops anywhere you'd notice.

H2

Routing Plan · Units, Ducts, Linesets, ERV

NOTHING CROSSES A ZONE LINE BUT TUBES

Returns: bedrooms breathe back to their zone's unit through wall transfer grilles with acoustic baffles (no panned joist bays — code and common sense agree). Kitchen: recirculating hood or a short ducted run out the rear wall — never up. ERV: pulls from both baths, laundry, and kitchen; supplies fresh to both bedrooms and living — one machine replaces every bath fan in the house.
