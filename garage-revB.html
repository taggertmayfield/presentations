It reads as a little sibling of Zone B. Floors switch from cassettes to a slab — you can't wash a truck on a wood floor.

Siting & Utility Run

TRENCH FROM HOUSE MECH ROOM

The garage sits east of the house, shifted forward so its man door lines up dead-on with the covered walk to the entry notch. The mechanical room at the house's rear east corner feeds a single ~45' trench carrying everything: power (60A feeder in 2" conduit — sized so a 48A EVSE and the wash gear can run together) and water (1" PEX below frost, ~36" cover in Richland County) to the rear utility wall. One trench, one inspection, both services.

Covered Walk · Garage → Entry Notch

35 LF · 5'-6" ROOF · POSTS @ 7'-0"

The walk runs dead straight from the garage man door to the entry notch — but its roof is held 5'-6" clear of Zone C's facade, a detached canopy on its own posts, so the Bedroom 2 window keeps its full light and view corridor. At the house end the canopy noses up to Zone B's 2'-0" overhang line; you step off the walk and you're already under the house's own roof at the door. Post spacing is the project's 7'-0" grid, and the canopy eave sits at 10'-0", matching the garage's west eave — one continuous datum from garage to house that also lifts the roof band to the window head and keeps the whole eye-level view open.
